Brit + Co. Diary Study

About the Project

Brit + Co. introduced a bookmarklet for its DIY community. The goal of this study was to explore the potential of bringing this bookmarklet to mobile.

Since the mobile experience is often unpredictable, I conducted a two-week diary study to help capture the experience. I recruited and worked with 7 participants who were all makers in the DIY community.

Week 1

Participants were asked to fill out a daily questionnaire relating to their mobile phone usage. The purpose of this exercise is to collect data on use cases and scenarios. Participants would share articles, notes, or pictures they bookmarked using their phones along with the scenario.

Week 2

The participants were asked to bookmark a collection of articles, blogs, or pictures using their smartphone. The purpose of this activity was to understand their current workflow and identify possible improvements.

Results

I analyzed the results using affinity diagrams. I created 3 personas, 3 scenarios, and a task analysis. I also identified success factors of popular tools and possible ways to improve the current workflow.
