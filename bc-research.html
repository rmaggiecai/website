About the Project

Brit + Co. introduced a bookmarklet that allows Brit + Co. users to collect content or inspiration from across the web. The goal of this study was to explore the potentials of bringing the bookmarklet to mobile by understanding their current mobile workflow.

Since the mobile experience is flexible and often unpredictable, I conducted a two-week diary study to help me better capture the experience. I recruited and worked with 7 participants, who were all representative of the Brit + Co. user base.

Week 1

To better understand users’ intentions and goals when they collect content or inspiration, participants were asked to fill out a daily questionnaire relating to their mobile phone usage. If they found new content or inspiration for their collection, I ask them to share the content with me and explain the scenario.

Week 2

The participants completed an activity that is very similar to using the bookmarklet. They each came up with a subject and were asked to collected content or inspiration surrounding that subject for the week. They also picked their favorite tool and completed this activity using their mobile phones. The purpose of this activity was to understand their current workflow and identify possible improvements.

Results

I analyzed the results using affinity diagrams. I created 3 personas, 3 scenarios, and a task flow. I also identified success factors of popular tools and possible ways to improve the current workflow.
