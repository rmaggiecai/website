About the Project

Extracurricular activities are an integral part of undergraduate experience. Currently, at the University of Toronto, students do not have a central medium to stay update on extracurricular events and opportunities. We set out to design a mobile app that keeps students updated on the latest extracurricular events and helps them stay involved in extracurriculars.

We used an iterative interaction design process to first understand the users, then design solution, and finally iterate on the proposed design.

User Research

We conducted interviews and surveys to understand students needs when it comes to getting involved in extracurricular activities. We spoke to 5 students in various years with different interests.

We created personas, scenarios, and uses cases to summarize and categorize what we learned from other students. We then took these information to these create requirements for the app design.

For my role, I led the user interviews, created personas, and drafted user requirements.

(abridged) Persona

Ashley Smith

2nd year Industrial Engineering student

Ashley is an ambitous, hard-working student. In addition to a 35-hour class schedule, she has assignments due every week. However, whenever she has some free time, she would try to attend extra- curricular events that interest her. She keeps her options open when it comes to extracurriculars. Sometimes she would attend professional events. Other times she would join clubs that matches interests, such as the SKULE volleyball team. She would attend event with friends or alone. If she attends an event not knowing anyone, she is able to make friends with the other attendees and expand her network.

Ashley's Goals

Have fun. School is tough, thus having a good balance is important to Ashley. These fun events breaks up her stressful schedule and are very important to her mental health.

Meet people and build a network. Ashley wants to expand her social network, especially expanding it outside of her discipline and year. “I like- meeting like-minded people who can challenge me and help me grow.”

Develop professionally. Ashley is very driven. She understands that if she wants to develop professionally she needs to be exposed to happenings in the industry and network with industry professionals.

Design

Through our user research, we learned that students get involved in extracurriculars often by first attending events. Furthermore, most students have very specific preferences and interests. Thus, we thought the best way to help students get involved in extracurricular is by recommending events and clubs based on their interests.

We started with sketches to brainstorm various features and functionalities of the app. We created low-fidelity wireframes using Basalmiq and later high-fidelity prototype using Sketch and InVision.
